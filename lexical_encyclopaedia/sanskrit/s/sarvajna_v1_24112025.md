## The Attested Sanskrit Analogue of the Tibetan "Kunkhyen" (ཀུན་མཁྱེན་): A Cross-Linguistic Analysis

> **Key Finding:**  
> The attested Sanskrit analogue of the Tibetan term **Kunkhyen** (ཀུན་མཁྱེན་), meaning "omniscient" or "all-knowing," is **sarvajña** (सर्वज्ञ). This equivalence is consistently documented in authoritative Sanskrit-Tibetan dictionaries, classical Buddhist texts, and is corroborated across all major Buddhist language traditions.

---

### Direct Answer

The Sanskrit term **sarvajña** (सर्वज्ञ), meaning "omniscient" or "all-knowing," is the attested and standard equivalent of the Tibetan "Kunkhyen" (ཀུན་མཁྱེན་). This equivalence is established in the Mahāvyutpatti (the principal Sanskrit-Tibetan Buddhist dictionary), confirmed by classical and modern lexicographical sources, and is reflected in the corresponding terms used in Pali, Chinese, Japanese, Mongolian, and Southeast Asian Buddhist traditions.

---

## Supporting Evidence and Cross-Linguistic Triangulation

### 1. Tibetan and Sanskrit: Lexicographical and Textual Evidence

- **Kunkhyen** (ཀུན་མཁྱེན་) is a compound meaning "all-knowing" or "omniscient," used as an honorific for Buddhas, Bodhisattvas, and great scholars .
- The **Mahāvyutpatti** and other authoritative dictionaries directly equate "Kunkhyen" with **sarvajña** (सर्वज्ञ) .
- **Sarvajña** is a compound of "sarva" (all) and "jña" (knowing), and is a standard epithet for the Buddha and enlightened beings in Sanskrit Buddhist literature.

### 2. Triangulation Across Buddhist Language Traditions

| Language/Tradition | Equivalent Term(s)                | Meaning/Usage                | Sanskrit Source                |
|--------------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Tibetan**        | ཀུན་མཁྱེན་ (kun mkhyen)         | Omniscient, all-knowing      | *sarvajña* (सर्वज्ञ)          |
| **Pali**           | sabbaññū                          | All-knowing, omniscient      | *sarvajña* (सर्वज्ञ)          |
| **Chinese**        | 一切智, 全知                       | Omniscient, all-knowing      | *sarvajña* (一切智)            |
| **Japanese**       | 一切智 (issai chi), 全知 (zenchi)  | Omniscient, all-knowing      | *sarvajña* (サルヴァジュニャ)   |
| **Mongolian**      | Бүгдийг мэдэгч                    | Knower of all, omniscient    | *sarvajña*                    |
| **Thai/Burmese**   | พระผู้รอบรู้ (Thai)               | Omniscient one               | *sarvajña*                    |

- **Pali:** *sabbaññū* is the direct Pali equivalent, derived from *sarvajña* .
- **Chinese:** 一切智 (yīqiè zhì, "all-knowing wisdom") and 全知 (quánzhī, "omniscient") are used for *sarvajña* .
- **Japanese:** 一切智 (issai chi) and 全知 (zenchi) are used, both tracing to *sarvajña* .
- **Mongolian:** Бүгдийг мэдэгч ("knower of all") is the standard translation for *sarvajña* .
- **Southeast Asian Languages:** Terms for "omniscient one" in Thai and Burmese correspond to *sarvajña*.

---

### 3. Usage and Context

- **Honorific and Doctrinal Use:**  
  "Kunkhyen" is used as an honorific for great Tibetan scholars (e.g., Longchen Rabjam, Jamgön Kongtrul) and as a doctrinal term for the omniscience of Buddhas and Bodhisattvas .
- **Canonical Attestation:**  
  The equivalence with *sarvajña* is found in canonical translation records, dictionaries, and is reflected in the doctrinal literature of all major Buddhist traditions.

## Conclusion

> **Key Takeaway:**  
> The Sanskrit term **sarvajña** (सर्वज्ञ) is the attested and universally recognized analogue of the Tibetan "Kunkhyen" (ཀུན་མཁྱེན་), as confirmed by primary lexicographical sources, canonical Buddhist texts, and consistent usage across all major Buddhist language traditions. This term encapsulates the concept of "omniscience" central to Buddhist doctrine and is the standard translation in both honorific and doctrinal contexts.
